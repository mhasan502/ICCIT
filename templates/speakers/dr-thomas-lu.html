Dr. Thomas Lu



Title: An Explainable AI Assistant for First Responder Driver Safety

Abstract: Fatalities caused by emergency responder vehicle collisions in the United States are 4.8 times higher than the national average. In this keynote presentation, an AI Assistant - Trusted and exPlainable Artificial Intelligence for Saving Lives (TruePAL) is presented. The TruePAL project was created as a result of U.S. Congressional Appropriations to study the technologies for saving first responders and roadside crews lives in and around active traffic. TruePAL provides real-time warning of risks that can be understood by a human driver. TruePAL analyzes data from multiple sources, performs reasoning and inferences, and provides the driver real-time prioritized action recommendations. TruePAL is capable of learning traffic history, analyzing the environment, using AI reasoning and applying traffic rules to generate timely warning to the drivers and roadside crews to avoid crashes. The TruePAL system has successfully demonstrated its capability of generating timely warning in over a dozen simulated traffic scenarios in busy intersections and roadsides. The preliminary test results have shown that TruePAL provides the drivers and crew members precious 3-7 seconds advanced warnings before a crash occurs.

Biography: Dr. Thomas Lu is a Senior Researcher at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, California Institute of Technology. His research focus has been in data analysis, artificial intelligence (AI), neural network architectures, deep learning, multispectral imaging, sensing and computer vision areas. He received JPL NASA Honor, Discovery, Voyager and Marina Awards for Innovations. He has served as an organizer and organizing committee member of SPIE conferences, edited a book on “Advancement in Patten Recognition Research”, contributed 3 book chapters, co-authored over 70 journal and conference papers, co-invented 6 patents. Dr. Lu received his Ph.D. degree in Electrical Engineering from the Pennsylvania State University.
